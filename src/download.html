How to install

  1. Open the DMG. Double-click Thunder-Kitty.dmg in your Downloads folder.
  2. Drag to Applications. Drag Thunder Kitty into your Applications folder.
  3. Launch and grant permissions. Open Thunder Kitty. You'll be asked to grant a handful of macOS permissions — here's what each one is for:
    • Microphone — to record you
    • Speech Recognition — to transcribe on-device
    • Screen Recording — to capture system audio from other apps (we don't record your screen)
    • Calendar (optional) — to show today's meetings in the sidebar
    • Notifications (optional) — to nudge you before meetings start

That's it. You're running Thunder Kitty entirely on your Mac.

Having trouble?

“Thunder Kitty can't be opened because Apple cannot check it for malicious software”

This is macOS being cautious about apps outside the App Store. Right-click Thunder Kitty in your Applications folder and choose Open . Click Open again in the dialog. You only have to do this once.

No audio is being captured

Make sure you've granted Microphone and Screen Recording permissions in System Settings → Privacy & Security . Quit and relaunch Thunder Kitty after granting access.

The app won't launch at all

Thunder Kitty requires macOS 26 (Tahoe) or later, on Apple Silicon. If you're on an earlier version of macOS, the app won't run on your machine. Support for macOS 14 and 15 is on the roadmap — drop us a line to be notified when it lands.

Something else

Email hello@thunderkitty.app . A human will reply.
